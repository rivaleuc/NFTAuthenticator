# NFTAuthenticator

NFT authenticity verification on GenLayer. AI validators check if artwork is original or copied, mint proof-of-authenticity badges.

## Why This Exists

NFT marketplaces are flooded with copied, stolen, and AI-generated art sold as originals. On-chain metadata proves nothing about authenticity — it only proves someone minted something. NFTAuthenticator uses AI validators to actually look at the art and assess originality.

## Why GenLayer

- **Visual understanding** — Validators can look at artwork and recognize style, composition, and similarity to known collections. Hash comparisons only catch exact duplicates.
- **Provenance reasoning** — "Is this a legitimate BAYC?" requires knowing what BAYC looks like, who minted the originals, and what contract they came from. AI validators have this contextual knowledge.
- **Catches derivatives and close copies** — Flipping an image, changing colors, or adding a filter fools hash-based systems. AI validators recognize the underlying work.
- **AI-generated art detection** — Validators can assess whether artwork was likely generated by AI models trained on existing collections — a growing problem in NFT markets.
- **Confidence scoring** — Not all assessments are binary. Validators return confidence levels, distinguishing "definitely stolen" from "stylistically similar but likely original."
